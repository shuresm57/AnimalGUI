Where I Live:

Crabs are fascinating creatures, with over 6,800 species found in oceans, freshwater habitats, and on land. These crustaceans have a hard exoskeleton, which provides them with protection from predators, and they are equipped with sharp claws used for defense and hunting. Many species of crabs are known for their sideways walk, a unique characteristic that allows them to move quickly across surfaces.

Crabs play a vital role in the marine ecosystem by helping with the decomposition of organic matter and being a key food source for many marine animals. On land, crabs contribute to the balance of coastal ecosystems by aerating the soil. In some cultures, crabs are harvested for their meat, which is considered a delicacy, while others are admired for their role in folklore and traditions.
