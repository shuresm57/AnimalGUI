Where I Live:

Dolphins are highly intelligent and social animals that are found in both freshwater and saltwater environments. Known for their playful behavior and acrobatic leaps, they are often seen riding the waves near boats. Dolphins have a complex communication system, using clicks, whistles, and body movements to interact with each other. Some species, like the bottlenose dolphin, are particularly well-studied due to their interactions with humans.

Dolphins are carnivorous and primarily feed on fish and squid. They have a specialized hunting technique known as "fish herding," where they work together to corral fish into tight groups, making them easier to catch. Due to their high intelligence and complex social structures, dolphins are often the subject of research in animal cognition, and their ability to understand tasks and exhibit problem-solving skills has made them a subject of fascination for scientists.
