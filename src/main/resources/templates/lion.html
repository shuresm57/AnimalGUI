Where I Live:

Lions, often called the "King of the Jungle," are one of the most iconic and powerful animals in the world. Known for their strength and majestic appearance, lions are apex predators in the African savannah. They live in prides, with males often serving as protectors of the group while females do most of the hunting. Lions primarily prey on large herbivores like wildebeest, zebras, and buffalo, using strategy and teamwork to bring down their prey.

Lions have been symbols of power, courage, and royalty throughout human history. In many cultures, they are seen as representations of strength and leadership. Unfortunately, lions are now endangered in many parts of their range, with habitat loss and poaching being significant threats. Conservation efforts are crucial to ensuring the survival of these magnificent creatures, which are now found mainly in protected areas across sub-Saharan Africa.
